3. What OnCall Does

OnCall is a peer-to-peer marketplace that allows nurses to post on-call shifts they need covered and allows other nurses to claim those shifts in exchange for a cash payment. OnCall facilitates the connection and payment between nurses — it does not employ nurses, direct clinical work, or guarantee shift coverage.

4. Payments and Refunds

When a shift is claimed, the posting nurse is charged the offer amount plus a platform and payment-processing fee. The exact total is always shown before you post a shift and before it is charged. The claiming nurse receives the full offer amount to their connected bank account after the shift ends. All payments are processed by Stripe. By using OnCall, you agree to Stripe's Terms of Service at stripe.com/legal.

A claiming nurse may withdraw from a claimed shift at any time before the shift starts using the Unclaim option in the app. In that case the posting nurse is automatically refunded in full and the shift reopens for others to claim.

Once a shift has started, payments are binding. OnCall does not guarantee refunds if a claiming nurse fails to cover a shift after it has started. Disputes between nurses are the responsibility of the nurses involved. OnCall may, at its discretion, assist in resolving disputes (including issuing refunds or reversing payouts in cases of confirmed no-shows) but is not obligated to do so.
